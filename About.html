FAQ

How much does a custom implant service cost?

The cost of a custom implant service depends on several factors, including case complexity, implant type, design requirements, materials, and production timeline. Each case is individually evaluated to ensure the best clinical and technical outcome. A detailed quotation will be provided after reviewing the patient data and surgical requirements.

What data do you need to start a case?

To start a case, we typically require the patient’s DICOM files (CT scan), along with any available STL files if applicable. A brief clinical description, surgical objectives, and any specific preferences from the surgeon are also important. This information allows our team to evaluate the case, plan the design accurately, and proceed efficiently.

What materials do you use for implants?

We use clinically proven, medical-grade materials selected based on the clinical indication and surgeon requirements. Commonly used materials include PEEK and medical-grade titanium, known for their strength, biocompatibility, and long-term performance. The final material is determined according to the case, anatomical location, and surgical objectives.
